Lada Kovler is a singer, musician, and educator whose repertoire spans jazz, pop, and folk traditions. Born and raised in Russia in a musical household—her mother a concert host and her father a symphony conductor—she trained in classical music from childhood before finding her true passion in jazz and popular song.

She has performed with jazz ensembles and big bands at numerous festivals and concerts, exploring styles from Turkmen folk to contemporary pop and jazz.

Lada founded Harmony, a private music school in Russia (2016–2020) that served over 300 students and produced seasonal concerts with live musicians.

After relocating to the United States, she earned a degree from Berklee College of Music in Interdisciplinary Music Studies and is currently pursuing an M.M. in Jazz Pedagogy at the University of Miami Frost School of Music.

She teaches voice and piano in Miami Beach and produces music for emerging artists, including Oskar Viniar.

Recording as Лада Виняр, Lada has released Russian-language singles on streaming platforms and in 2026 released the single "Waiting for My Miracle."

Family is central to her life—she lives in Miami Beach with her husband, two sons, and their dog, Grammy.
